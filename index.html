Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to the most common concerns about immigration legal help.

Is it safe to contact you if I am undocumented?

Yes, it is completely safe. Many of the people we help do not have immigration status. Contacting us does not put you at risk. We do not report clients to immigration authorities or work with the government against our clients. Our role is to protect you, guide you, and explain your legal options safely and confidentially.

What happens during my first consultation?

During the consultation, we listen to your story with respect, review your documents, and analyze your immigration situation. We explain your real options, possible risks, and next steps. You are never forced to make decisions or hire services on the same day.

What should I do if immigration contacts me or I receive a letter?

Do not sign, respond, or make decisions without legal advice. Many people make mistakes out of fear or confusion. Contact us immediately so we can review the letter or call and explain the safest next steps.

Will you explain the risks of my case?

Yes. We believe honesty is essential. We explain both the benefits and risks of your case so you can make informed and responsible decisions. We never hide important information.

What makes your service different from others?

Our approach is human and responsible. We treat every case with respect, explain everything clearly, and never promise unrealistic results. Our goal is to protect you and support you with integrity.
